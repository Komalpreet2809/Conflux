The Problem

Bengaluru events break the city —
and we plan for them reactively.

Cricket at Chinnaswamy, concerts at Palace Grounds, rallies at Freedom Park, marathons, sudden protests — each one chokes carriageways and junctions for hours.
  • Impact isn't quantified in advance. Officers don't know which junctions will fail, or when.
  • Resource deployment is experience-driven. Manpower, barricades and diversions are decided by gut, not data.
  • No post-event learning system. The same mistakes repeat — nothing is measured against what actually happened.
Conflux02
The Solution

Forecast → Recommend → Learn

One workflow, mapped 1:1 to the problem statement.
01 · Forecast

See it before it happens

ML predicts per-junction congestion over the full event timeline — arrival, peak, dispersal — isolating the event's own impact from baseline traffic.

02 · Recommend

Turn it into a plan

Optimal manpower allocation, barricade points, and diversion routes — rendered live on the map, sized to a real staffing budget.

03 · Learn

Get sharper each time

Predicted-vs-actual replay of past events with accuracy metrics — the post-event learning loop, built in.

Under the Hood · Honest ML

A model that actually learns

Pillar 2 · Recommend

From forecast to field plan

Manpower

Where the officers go

Greedy marginal-utility allocation against a staffing budget — prioritising event-surge × congestion × network centrality, with expected delay reduction per junction.

Barricades

Control the inflow

Inflow-restriction points on the worst corridors near the venue, scored by impact and proximity.

Diversions

Keep the city moving

Congestion-aware re-routing of cross-city through-traffic around the event zone — computed on the live road graph.
